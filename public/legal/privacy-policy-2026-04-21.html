If we take an adverse action with respect to your application or tenancy (for example, denying an application, requiring a higher deposit or guarantor, or otherwise conditioning approval) based in whole or in part on information contained in a consumer report, we will provide you with an adverse action notice as required by the FCRA and applicable state law. That notice will identify the consumer reporting agency that supplied the report, inform you that the agency did not make the decision and cannot explain the specific reasons for it, and advise you of your right to obtain a free copy of your report from that agency and to dispute the accuracy or completeness of any information in it.

4.3. Options for Opting out of Cookies and Mobile Device Identifiers

If we process Cookies based on your consent (as indicated at the time of collection of such data), you have the right to withdraw your consent at any point in time by contacting us at admin@amrikahousing.com. Please note, that if you exercise this right, you may have to then provide your consent on a case-by-case basis for the use by us or disclosure of certain Cookies, if such use or disclosure is necessary to enable you to utilize some or all of our services.

Some web browsers allow you to reject Cookies or to alert you when a Cookie is placed on your computer, tablet or mobile device. You may be able to reject mobile device identifiers by activating the appropriate setting on your mobile device. Although you are not required to accept Amrika Housing's Cookies or mobile device identifiers, if you block or reject them, you may not have access to all features available through the Amrika Housing Services.

  • You may opt out of receiving certain Cookies and certain trackers by visiting the Network Advertising Initiative (NAI) opt out page or the Digital Advertising Alliance (DAA), or by installing the DAA's AppChoice app on your mobile computing device. When you use these opt-out features, an "opt-out" Cookie will be placed on your computer, tablet or mobile computing device indicating that you do not want to receive interest-based advertising from NAI or DAA member companies. If you delete Cookies on your computer, tablet or mobile computing device, you may need to opt out again. For information about how to opt out of interest-based advertising on mobile devices, you may consider third party sources such as Applicationprivacy.org. You will need to opt out of each browser and device for which you desire to apply these opt-out features.
  • Even after opting out of Behavioral Advertising, you may still see Amrika Housing advertisements that are not interest-based (i.e., not targeted toward you). Also, opting out does not mean that Amrika Housing is no longer using Tracking Tools — Amrika Housing still may collect information about your use of the Amrika Housing Services even after you have opted out of Behavioral Advertising and may still serve advertisements to you via the Amrika Housing Services based on information it collects via the Amrika Housing Services.

This Privacy Policy does not cover the use of Cookies and other Tracking Tools by any third parties, and we aren't responsible for their privacy policies and practices. Please be aware that some Cookies placed by third parties can continue to track your activities online even after you have left the Amrika Housing Services.

4.4. How Amrika Housing Responds to Browser "Do Not Track" (DNT) and Universal Opt-Out Preference Signals

Some web browsers (including Safari, Internet Explorer, Firefox and Chrome) incorporate a "Do Not Track" (DNT) or similar feature that signals to web services that a visitor does not want to have their online activity and behavior tracked. Historically, there has not been consensus on what constitutes a DNT signal, so many web sites do not honor them, including this one.

New standards are being developed for a Universal Opt-Out Preference Signal, such as the Global Privacy Control (GPC), which allow users with GPC-enabled browsers and devices to send a signal that will communicate the user's request to opt-out of sales of their personal information and to opt-out of certain sharing of their personal information. The CPRA and other laws allow for the acceptance of Opt-Out Preference Signals such as the GPC, as an option for users to transmit an Opt-Out of selling/sharing personal information. Our website is designed to recognize such a signal from your device or browser. As an alternative, if you wish to opt-out of the selling or sharing of your personal information for these purposes, please access the following link: DO NOT SELL/SHARE MY DATA [INSERT LINK].

8.4 Privacy Rights for Residents of Other U.S. States

In addition to the California rights described above, a growing number of U.S. states have enacted comprehensive consumer privacy laws. If you are a resident of a U.S. state that grants privacy rights to its residents, you may have some or all of the rights described in this Section 8.4, in each case only to the extent provided by, and subject to the exceptions and verification requirements of, the law of your state of residence. The specific rights available to you, the categories of information covered, and the thresholds that determine whether a right applies depend on your state's law. Where your state's law does not grant a particular right, that right does not apply to you.

Rights that may be available to you. Depending on your state of residence, you may have the right to:

  • Confirm and access. Confirm whether we are processing your personal information and request access to that information.
  • Correct. Request that we correct inaccurate personal information we maintain about you.
  • Delete. Request that we delete personal information we have collected from or about you.
  • Portability. Obtain a copy of your personal information in a portable and, to the extent technically feasible, readily usable format.
  • Opt out of "sale." Direct us not to sell your personal information.
  • Opt out of "targeted advertising" / "sharing." Direct us not to process your personal information for targeted advertising or to share it for cross-context behavioral advertising.
  • Opt out of profiling. Opt out of profiling in furtherance of decisions that produce legal or similarly significant effects concerning you (where your state's law provides this right).
  • Limit the use of sensitive personal information. Limit our use and disclosure of your sensitive personal information to that which is necessary to provide the services you have requested.
  • Non-discrimination. Exercise any of your rights without receiving discriminatory or retaliatory treatment.

Sensitive personal information. Many state laws treat certain categories of data — such as Social Security and other government identifiers, financial account information, precise geolocation, and data revealing characteristics like race, religion, health, or immigration status — as "sensitive." Unlike California's opt-out approach, several states require your affirmative opt-in consent before we may process your sensitive personal information for certain purposes. Where your state's law requires it, we will obtain your consent before processing your sensitive personal information for those purposes, or will provide a means for you to limit such processing. At least one state (Maryland) prohibits the sale of sensitive personal information altogether; where applicable, we will not sell your sensitive personal information.

Universal opt-out / Global Privacy Control. As described in Section 4.4, our website is designed to recognize an Opt-Out Preference Signal, such as the Global Privacy Control (GPC). A number of states require businesses to honor such signals as a valid request to opt out of the sale of personal information and/or processing for targeted advertising. Where applicable law requires it, we will treat a recognized signal from your browser or device as a request to opt out for the device or browser from which it is sent.

How to exercise your rights. To exercise any of the rights above, please submit a request using the contact information in Section 13, or, for opt-out rights, by using the DO NOT SELL/SHARE MY DATA [INSERT LINK] and PRIVACY REQUEST [INSERT LINK] links. To protect your information, we will take reasonable steps to verify your identity before fulfilling a request, and we may decline to act on a request, or charge a reasonable fee, where permitted by law (for example, for manifestly unfounded or excessive requests). You may use an authorized agent to submit a request on your behalf, subject to our verification of the agent's authority and, where required, of your identity.

Right to appeal. Several states give you the right to appeal our refusal to act on a request. If we decline to take action on your request and your state's law provides an appeal right, we will inform you of our decision and the reasons for it, and you may appeal by contacting us using the information in Section 13. Within the timeframe required by your state's law, we will inform you in writing of any action taken or not taken in response to the appeal, including the reasons for our decision. If your appeal is denied, you may have the right to contact your state Attorney General to submit a complaint.
